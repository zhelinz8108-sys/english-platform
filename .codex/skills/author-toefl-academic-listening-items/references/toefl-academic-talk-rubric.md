# TOEFL Academic Listening item-writing rubric

## Set design

- Produce exactly four single-choice questions with options a, b, c, and d.
- Select types from main_idea, detail, rhetorical_purpose, inference, organization, and
  prediction. Do not force one of every type.
- Include at least one of rhetorical_purpose, inference, organization, or prediction.
- Use no more than two detail questions.
- Distribute evidence across the beginning, middle, and end of the full transcript.
- Do not require a vocabulary question. Use vocabulary only when meaning-in-context is central
  to comprehension, and classify it under detail or inference.

## Prompt construction

- Test the relationship among ideas, not isolated terminology or outside facts.
- Keep prompts concise and idiomatic. Use formulations such as main topic, purpose, why the
  speaker mentions an example, what can be inferred, how the talk is organized, or what is
  likely to follow.
- Refer to the actual source accurately: talk, discussion, program, presenter, or speakers.
  Do not invent a professor, class, student, or visual that is absent from the audio.
- Make the correct answer a defensible paraphrase rather than a copied sentence.

## Distractors

- Keep all four options grammatically parallel and at a similar level of specificity.
- Derive distractors from nearby ideas, reversed relationships, incorrect scope, or plausible
  misinterpretations of the transcript.
- Avoid absurd options, answer-length giveaways, repeated wording unique to the correct
  option, mutually overlapping answers, and two answers that can both be defended.
- Do not use all/never/only unless the transcript supports that absolute claim.

## Evidence and explanations

- Record exact transcript start and end character offsets. The quote must equal the substring
  at those offsets.
- Use the smallest evidence span that fully supports the answer; add a second span only when
  integration across ideas is essential.
- Explain the correct answer in Chinese and explain why each distractor fails. Do not merely
  translate the option.
- Flag an item rather than inventing evidence when the source does not support a unique answer.

## Difficulty

- Label audio and item difficulty as low, medium, or high only as provisional judgments.
- Raise item difficulty through paraphrase, inference distance, discourse function, and
  integration across statements, not through obscure outside knowledge.
- Do not claim psychometric calibration, official ETS difficulty, or score equivalence.
